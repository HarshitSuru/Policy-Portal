Heritage City Development and Augmentation Yojana (HRIDAY)

HRIDAY was launched in January 2015 with the objective of preserving and revitalizing the heritage character of 12 heritage cities across India. The focus areas include revitalizing urban infrastructure such as water supply, sanitation, drainage, and waste management systems in the heritage zones. It also aims to promote adaptive reuse of heritage buildings and improving connectivity to heritage sites. The scheme encourages active participation of local communities and stakeholders in the preservation and management of cultural heritage.

National Mission on Libraries

The National Mission on Libraries was launched in 2012 to modernize and digitally connect public and school libraries across India. The mission aims to provide equitable access to information and knowledge resources, especially in remote and underserved areas. It supports the creation of a National Virtual Library of India (NVLI) to provide access to digital resources, e-books, journals, and other digital content. The mission also focuses on capacity building of librarians and promoting library-based learning among students and the general public.

Scheme for Promotion of Culture of Science (SPOCS)

SPOCS aims to promote a scientific temperament among citizens, particularly youth, by organizing and supporting science-related events such as exhibitions, workshops, and popular lectures. It encourages innovation, creativity, and critical thinking in scientific pursuits and aims to bridge the gap between scientific knowledge and societal applications. The scheme also supports the establishment of science clubs, science communication initiatives, and collaborative projects between scientific institutions and communities.

Rashtriya Sanskriti Mahotsav

The Rashtriya Sanskriti Mahotsav is a cultural festival organized by the Ministry of Culture to celebrate India's rich cultural diversity. It showcases traditional art forms, music, dance, crafts, and cuisine from different regions of the country. The festival aims to promote national integration by highlighting the cultural heritage of various communities and encouraging cultural exchange among states and regions. It provides a platform for artists and performers to showcase their talents and for visitors to experience the cultural vibrancy of India.

National Cultural Fund (NCF)

The National Cultural Fund was established to support projects and initiatives related to the preservation, promotion, and dissemination of India's cultural heritage. It provides financial assistance for conservation and restoration of heritage sites, museums, libraries, and cultural artifacts. The fund also supports research, documentation, and publication of cultural resources, as well as initiatives to revive traditional art forms and promote cultural education. It plays a crucial role in safeguarding India's cultural heritage for future generations.

Scheme for Safeguarding the Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH)

Launched by the Ministry of Culture, this scheme focuses on safeguarding and promoting India's intangible cultural heritage (ICH). Intangible cultural heritage includes oral traditions, performing arts, rituals, festive events, knowledge and practices concerning nature and the universe, and traditional craftsmanship. The scheme aims to create awareness about the significance of ICH and support communities, groups, and individuals involved in its preservation. It encourages documentation, research, and dissemination of ICH practices to ensure their continuity and sustainability. Additionally, the scheme promotes capacity building among stakeholders and facilitates the nomination of elements of ICH for inclusion in UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
