Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) was launched on August 28, 2014, with the objective of ensuring financial inclusion for all households in India. The scheme aims to provide financial services such as savings accounts, remittances, credit, insurance, and pension to the unbanked population. By offering a zero-balance savings account with easy access to banking facilities, PMJDY aims to bring the marginalized sections of society into the formal banking system. The scheme also promotes direct benefit transfers (DBT) to ensure efficient delivery of subsidies and benefits to beneficiaries. As of the latest data, PMJDY has made significant strides, with millions of accounts opened across the country, particularly benefiting women and economically weaker sections.

pmjdy.gov.in

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)

The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) was enacted in 2005 to provide livelihood security to rural households by guaranteeing 100 days of wage employment in a financial year to every household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. The primary objective of MGNREGA is to enhance the livelihood security of rural households by generating employment opportunities in rural areas, thereby reducing rural-urban migration. The scheme also aims to create durable assets and strengthen the social safety net for vulnerable sections of society, especially women, Scheduled Castes (SCs), and Scheduled Tribes (STs). MGNREGA has been instrumental in improving the socio-economic status of rural households by providing them with a stable source of income and empowering them to participate in local developmental activities. The official website of MGNREGA offers detailed information about the scheme's implementation, guidelines, operational processes, success stories, and grievance redressal mechanisms.

nrega.nic.in

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP) is a flagship initiative launched on January 22, 2015, by the Government of India to address the declining Child Sex Ratio (CSR) and empower the girl child. The scheme aims to change societal mindsets and create awareness about the importance of educating and nurturing girls. BBBP focuses on three critical areas: preventing gender-biased sex selection, ensuring survival and protection of the girl child, and ensuring education and participation of the girl child. The scheme includes multi-sectoral interventions such as enforcement of Pre-Conception and Pre-Natal Diagnostic Techniques (PCPNDT) Act, provision of incentives for girl children, promoting education and skills among girls, and creating an enabling environment for their development. BBBP has been implemented through a convergent approach involving ministries of Women and Child Development, Health and Family Welfare, and Human Resource Development, along with active participation of state governments, local bodies, and community stakeholders. The official BBBP website provides comprehensive information about the scheme's objectives, strategies, implementation guidelines, success stories, and resources for stakeholders.

wcd.nic.in/bbbp-schemes

Digital India

Digital India is a flagship initiative launched by the Government of India to transform India into a digitally empowered society and knowledge economy. Launched on July 1, 2015, Digital India aims to ensure that government services are made available to citizens electronically by improving online infrastructure and increasing internet connectivity. The initiative focuses on digital literacy, digital infrastructure, e-governance, and promoting digital transactions and cybersecurity. Digital India aims to bridge the digital divide by empowering citizens through digital inclusion and promoting digital empowerment at all levels of society. The official Digital India website provides comprehensive information about the initiative's objectives, key initiatives, e-services, digital literacy programs, and updates on digital infrastructure projects across the country.

www.digitalindia.gov.in

Swachh Bharat Mission

The Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) was launched on October 2, 2014, with the aim of achieving universal sanitation coverage and making India open defecation free by October 2, 2019, commemorating the 150th birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi. SBM focuses on constructing household toilets, community and public toilets, solid waste management, and behavioral change regarding sanitation practices. The mission has two sub-components: Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) for rural areas and Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban) for urban areas. SBM has significantly contributed to improving sanitation infrastructure and promoting cleanliness and hygiene across India. The official SBM website provides comprehensive information about the mission's objectives, guidelines, progress dashboard, best practices, success stories, and resources for stakeholders. It also facilitates online monitoring and reporting of sanitation activities and achievements at the national, state, and district levels.

swachhbharatmission.ddws.gov.in

Skill India Mission

Skill India Mission, launched on July 15, 2015, by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, aims to empower the youth of the country with skill development training to enhance their employability and entrepreneurship opportunities. The mission focuses on providing industry-relevant skill training to youth across India through various skill development programs, certification courses, apprenticeships, and recognition of prior learning (RPL). Skill India aims to bridge the gap between demand and supply of skilled workforce across sectors and promote inclusive growth by empowering individuals with relevant skills for employment and self-employment. The official Skill India website provides detailed information about the mission's objectives, skill development programs, training partners, job roles, certification processes, and initiatives to promote entrepreneurship. It also offers resources for skill seekers, employers, training providers, and updates on skill development initiatives and achievements.

www.skillindiadigital.gov.in

National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP)

The National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched in 1995 to provide financial assistance to elderly persons, widows, and disabled individuals living below the poverty line. NSAP comprises five sub-schemes: Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS), Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS), Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (IGNDPS), National Family Benefit Scheme (NFBS), and Annapurna Scheme. The program aims to provide social security and ensure a dignified life for vulnerable sections of society by supplementing their income. NSAP is implemented by state governments and union territories, and funds are transferred directly to beneficiaries' bank accounts. The official NSAP website provides detailed information about each sub-scheme, eligibility criteria, application process, benefits, and grievance redressal mechanisms. It also offers updates on scheme implementation, beneficiary statistics, and guidelines for stakeholders.

nsap.nic.in
