Ayushman Bharat Yojana (PM-JAY)

Launched in 2018, Ayushman Bharat is a flagship health insurance scheme aimed at providing coverage of up to ₹5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalization. This scheme targets over 10 crore economically vulnerable families, approximately 50 crore beneficiaries, making it the world's largest government-funded health insurance program. Ayushman Bharat seeks to reduce out-of-pocket healthcare expenses, strengthen healthcare infrastructure, and improve access to quality healthcare services across India. Beneficiaries can avail cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals, ensuring financial protection during times of medical emergencies.

pmjay.gov.in

National Health Mission (NHM)

NHM is a comprehensive health program launched to provide accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare to both rural and urban populations in India. It encompasses the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) and the National Urban Health Mission (NUHM), focusing on maternal and child health, communicable diseases, non-communicable diseases, sanitation, and hygiene. NHM aims to strengthen health infrastructure, enhance human resources for health, implement health information systems, and promote community participation in health initiatives. This mission plays a crucial role in addressing healthcare disparities and improving health outcomes nationwide.

nhm.gov.in

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)

PMMVY is a maternity benefit program designed to provide partial compensation for wage loss to pregnant women and lactating mothers during their first live birth. The scheme offers a direct benefit transfer of ₹5,000 to eligible women, promoting proper nutrition and health check-ups before and after childbirth. PMMVY aims to ensure healthy pregnancies and safe deliveries, thereby reducing maternal and neonatal mortality rates. Women can access detailed information, including eligibility criteria and application processes

pmmvy.wcd.gov.in

National AIDS Control Programme (NACP)

NACP is a comprehensive program aimed at preventing new HIV infections and reducing AIDS-related mortality and morbidity in India. The program focuses on prevention strategies among high-risk groups, awareness campaigns, HIV testing services, and treatment with antiretroviral therapy (ART) for people living with HIV/AIDS (PLHIV). By integrating prevention, care, support, and treatment services, NACP plays a crucial role in the national response to HIV/AIDS. Stakeholders and beneficiaries can find updates, resources, and program details on the National AIDS Control Programme

naco.gov.in

National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP)

NTCP is a strategic initiative to control tobacco consumption and reduce tobacco-related diseases in India. The program implements tobacco control laws, conducts awareness campaigns, promotes cessation services, and monitors tobacco use trends. NTCP aims to protect people from exposure to tobacco smoke, prevent initiation of tobacco use, and promote quitting among current users through evidence-based interventions.

ntcp.mohfw.gov.in

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

RBSK focuses on providing comprehensive healthcare to children from birth to 18 years through early detection and management of various health issues. The program aims to screen children for 4 Ds - Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Diseases, and Development delays including Disabilities. By identifying health problems early, RBSK facilitates timely interventions and referrals to appropriate healthcare facilities, thereby improving child health outcomes across India.

rbsk.mohfw.gov.in

Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY)

PMSSY aims to correct regional imbalances in the availability of affordable and reliable tertiary healthcare services in India. The scheme supports setting up AIIMS-like institutions, upgrading existing government medical colleges, and strengthening healthcare infrastructure across the country. PMSSY enhances access to specialized medical education, advanced healthcare services, and research facilities, contributing to the overall improvement of healthcare delivery in underserved regions.

pmssy.mohfw.gov.in
