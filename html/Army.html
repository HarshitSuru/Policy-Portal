Ex-Servicemen Contributory Health Scheme (ECHS)

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) was launched on August 28, 2014, with the objective of ensuring financial inclusion for all households in India. The scheme aims to provide financial services such as savings accounts, remittances, credit, insurance, and pension to the unbanked population. By offering a zero-balance savings account with easy access to banking facilities, PMJDY aims to bring the marginalized sections of society into the formal banking system. The scheme also promotes direct benefit transfers (DBT) to ensure efficient delivery of subsidies and benefits to beneficiaries. As of the latest data, PMJDY has made significant strides, with millions of accounts opened across the country, particularly benefiting women and economically weaker sections.

www.echs.gov.in

National Defence Fund (NDF)

The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) was enacted in 2005 to provide livelihood security to rural households by guaranteeing 100 days of wage employment in a financial year to every household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. The primary objective of MGNREGA is to enhance the livelihood security of rural households by generating employment opportunities in rural areas, thereby reducing rural-urban migration. The scheme also aims to create durable assets and strengthen the social safety net for vulnerable sections of society, especially women, Scheduled Castes (SCs), and Scheduled Tribes (STs). MGNREGA has been instrumental in improving the socio-economic status of rural households by providing them with a stable source of income and empowering them to participate in local developmental activities. The official website of MGNREGA offers detailed information about the scheme's implementation, guidelines, operational processes, success stories, and grievance redressal mechanisms.

ndf.gov.in

Agnipath Scheme

Agnipath Scheme (also spelled Agneepath Scheme) (Hindi: Agnīpath Yojanā, transl. Agnipath Scheme) is a tour of duty style scheme approved by the Government of India on 14 June 2022 and implemented in the country a few months later in September 2022, for recruitment of soldiers below the rank of commissioned officers into the three services of the armed forces. All recruits will be hired only for a four year period. Personnel recruited under this system are to be called Agniveers (transl. Fire-Warriors), which will be a new military rank. The introduction of the scheme has been criticised for lack of consultation and public debate. This scheme will bypass many things including long tenures, pension and other benefits which were there in old system. Opposition parties in India have criticised and expressed concerns about the consequences of the new scheme. They have asked the scheme to be put on hold and that the scheme be discussed in the Parliament.

Prime Minister’s Scholarship Scheme (PMSS)

This scheme provides scholarships for the wards and widows of deceased or ex-service personnel. It aims to encourage technical and post-graduate education in fields such as engineering, medicine, and management. Scholarships amount to ₹2,500 per month for boys and ₹3,000 per month for girls​ (Department of Ex-servicemen Welfare)​.

www.desw.gov.in/prime-ministers-scholarship-scheme-pmss

Armed Forces Welfare Housing Organization (AFWHO)

This organization facilitates housing for serving and retired defense personnel. It negotiates with builders to provide housing units at affordable prices. The AFWHO does not develop properties but ensures that its members get quality homes at reduced rates​ (AFWHO)​.
